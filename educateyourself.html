Texting while driving is a danger because it leads to many accidents and deaths and many people aren’t even aware that it’s a danger. Texting while driving can make an accident twenty three times more likely to happen. Many people think that sending one quick text wont distract them for long enough for an accident to occur, but five seconds is the minimum time needed to send a text, and while you’re sending that text if you’re going on a highway you will travel more than the length of one football field before you’re done. Texting while driving can cause injuries and deaths in a larger scale then many people think. In 2009, nearly 5,500 people died and half a million were injured in crashes involving a distracted driver. If your car is not insured then you will pay a lot of money if your texting while driving and get in an accident. There are a few texting while driving PSAs, but not as many as there should be and the ones that are there just say don’t do it.

No text is worth your life.

There are three ways that you can help the Texting While Driving cause. One, just don't do it. Put your phone away while you're on the road.

Two, to help you with that, download AT&T's free Drive Mode app.

Third, now that you know about it, tell other people that you know. Visit the Spread the Word page to start.

  • Texting impairs the ability of drivers to maintain proper lane position and a constant speed.
  • Text messaging is banned for all drivers in 39 states and the District of Columbia. In addition, novice drivers are banned from texting in 5 states (Mississippi, Missouri, New Mexico, Oklahoma, and Texas) and school bus drivers are banned from text messaging in 3 states (Mississippi, Oklahoma, and Texas)
  • According to researchers from the University of North Texas Health Science Center in Ft. Worth, texting behind the wheel accounted for 16,141 deaths between 2002 and 2007
  • The percentage of all traffic deaths caused by distracted driving rose from 11% in 1999 to 16% in 2008
  • In 2011 at least 23% of auto collisions involved cell phones
  • 5 seconds is thee minimum amount of time your attention is taken away from the road when you’re texting while driving
